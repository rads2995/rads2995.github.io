EML 3034C - Modeling Methods in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering

Numerical computational methods applied to Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. Topics include solution methods, curve fitting, optimization, computational heat transfer and fluid flow, inverse problems, boundary elements, and meshless methods. Concepts are delivered using industry leading numerical computing environments and/or programming languages, such as Julia, Python, Mathcad, and Matlab.

About Me

My name is Rodrigo Duran. I am a mechanical engineering graduate student working for the mechanical and aerospace engineering department at the University of Central Florida.

My long term goal is to conduct scientific research as a guidance, navigation, and control software developer with a focus on advanced algorithm design, modeling and simulation, control theory, robotics and autonomous systems.
